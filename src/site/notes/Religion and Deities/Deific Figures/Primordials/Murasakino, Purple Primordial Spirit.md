# Murasakino, Purple Primordial Spirit
*"Lord of Dusk and Dawn" • "Creator of Magic"*

## Overview

Murasakino (MOO-rah-sah-KEE-no), Lord of Dusk and Dawn, is the primordial spirit fabled to have witnessed [[Places/Materia\|Materia]]'s very creation, standing present at the moment when formless potential became structured reality. They embody the concept of change itself—not gradual evolution but the transformative moments when one state becomes another, when day surrenders to night and night yields to morning, when the mundane crosses the threshold into the magical. It is said that Murasakino granted the greatest gift ever bestowed upon mortals: magic itself, bestowed millennia ago when the primordial judged mortal races ready to wield power that could reshape reality. This decision fundamentally altered the world's trajectory, demonstrating that Murasakino values potential and transformation above stability or tradition.

Transitions and thresholds—these are Murasakino's domain. The moment of dusk when day has ended but night has not yet begun. The instant of dawn when darkness gives way to light. The depths of caves where surface world meets subterranean mysteries. The instant when a spell transforms thought into reality. The [[Species and Cultures/Races/Trox\|Trox]] maintain particularly strong devotion to Murasakino, as their race emerged from caves into surface world during their own transformation, crossing a threshold that defined their existence. Those who worship Murasakino understand that change is not merely inevitable but sacred, that resistance to transformation is resistance to nature's fundamental design, and that magic itself is proof that reality can be altered by those wise enough to attempt it.

## Worship and Clergy

### Domains
**Granted Domains:** Earth, Luck, Madness, Magic

**Subdomains:** Caves, Fate, Metal, Truth

### Holy Symbol
A circle divided by a horizontal line, purple above and brown below—representing the horizon at dusk or dawn, the threshold where sky meets earth and day transforms into night or night into day.

### Sacred Elements
**Sacred Animal:** Raven
**Sacred Colors:** Purple and Brown

### Worshipers

Magic casters who recognize the primordial as the source of their power, and [[Species and Cultures/Races/Trox\|Trox]] who venerate the spirit tied to their ancestral emergence from underground realms, make up Murasakino's faithful. Sacred spaces are found in caves where surface transitions to depths, at horizons where earth meets sky, and in places where magic naturally concentrates. The clergy provides no comfort to those fearing change but rather teaches that transformation is opportunity, that clinging to familiar forms is a slow death, and that the universe rewards those brave enough to cross thresholds into the unknown.

Ravens thrive in both day and night, appear equally comfortable in multiple environments, and demonstrate the intelligence required to adapt and transform. Seen at dusk and dawn—transitional times when Murasakino's power is strongest—their presence is interpreted as the primordial's watchful eye observing whether mortals continue to prove worthy of the magic they possess.

Priests conduct rituals at transitional times: dawn, dusk, equinoxes, and solstices, when the world itself demonstrates change's inevitability. They teach magic to those with aptitude, viewing education as sacred duty since magic is Murasakino's direct legacy to mortals. The clergy studies magic not merely as a tool but as communion with Murasakino's gift, each spell a small echo of the primordial's power to reshape reality. They maintain extensive records of magical discoveries, believing that each new spell or technique represents mortals proving themselves worthy. The faith teaches that change is sacred, that thresholds and transitions hold power that stable states cannot match, and that magic was granted not as a gift but as a test—proof that mortals could be trusted with the ability to alter reality itself. Murasakino appears at moments of transformation, watching to see if mortals will embrace change or cling desperately to what was.

### Tenets of Faith

To follow Murasakino requires adherence to these beliefs:

1. **Change is sacred, resistance is futile.** I shall embrace transformation rather than cling to what was.

2. **Magic is not mine by right but by trust.** I shall prove worthy of the gift Murasakino granted when mortals were judged ready.

3. **Thresholds hold power that stable states cannot match.** I shall seek moments of transition, for in them lies the potential for transformation.

4. **The raven knows both day and night.** I shall adapt to all circumstances, finding opportunity in every transition.

5. **Reality can be altered by those wise enough to attempt it.** I shall not accept the world as unchangeable, for magic itself proves otherwise.

## Holy Days and Festivals

### The Day of Two Transitions

Twice on the autumn equinox—once at dawn, once at dusk—Murasakino's followers honor change itself. When day and night achieve perfect balance before the world tips toward darkness, they gather at horizons or cave mouths to witness both transformative moments.

Dawn: Followers position themselves to catch the exact moment of transition from darkness to light. The clergy leads meditations on transformations experienced during the past year—skills learned, places left behind, relationships that ended, beliefs that changed. Magic casters demonstrate spells learned during this time, each casting a tribute to Murasakino's gift and proof that mortals continue evolving. [[Species and Cultures/Races/Trox\|Trox]] participants share stories of their ancestors' emergence from caves, connecting their race's greatest transformation to the daily miracle of dawn. Ravens are released at sunrise, their flight symbolizing the soul's willingness to embrace change rather than remain in comfortable darkness.

The day between transitions is spent examining what transformations the coming darker season might bring. Caves are explored, horizons traveled to, magical research conducted—all activities that honor thresholds and change.

Dusk: Followers return to witness light surrender to darkness. The evening ritual focuses on accepting that not all change appears positive initially—the coming darkness enables rest, reflection, and the preparations necessary for spring's eventual renewal. Just as magic allows mortals to transform reality, each individual possesses the power to transform themselves. Both dusk and dawn prove that endings are merely transitions to new states rather than final conclusions. The day concludes in darkness, with participants acknowledging they cannot see what comes next but trusting that transformation will continue as it always has.
